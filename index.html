What connects them

Three fronts. One through-line.

The constraint is different on each front. The discipline is the same: find the path through, not around. That's what Front Lines of Code teaches — practical capability, built from real problems, with no shortcuts that don't hold up under pressure.

The Wire

Locked-down networks where modern tools don't reach

Zone B's air gap is a real constraint — not a configuration problem to talk your way around. Developers inside DoD networks who want to use AI need a different map than the one YouTube tutorials assume. This front owns that map.

The Craft

AI as a teammate, not a search engine with better grammar

There's a gap between using autocomplete and actually working with an AI agent as a collaborator. Crossing it requires understanding context, memory, and how to delegate — not just knowing which tools exist. This front teaches the crossing.

The Home

Families navigating AI without a framework

Parents are making high-stakes decisions about AI with their kids every day, mostly alone. Schools are silent or vague. The tools are powerful and the privacy questions are real. This front is for the parents who want to figure it out before the default choice gets made for them.

About

A mathematician who ended up rebuilding a system in a war zone

In 2010, I was the "math guy" in charge of tracking 100,000 US troops in Afghanistan at the peak of the surge. The systems we had weren't built for what was being demanded at the front lines, so I learned to code in ways people could actually use, and rebuilt them. By the time I left, my office ran the mission with fewer than half the personnel it started with — and the system stayed in use across Afghanistan for almost a decade.

That's the pattern I keep repeating. A PhD in dynamical systems and computational neuroscience led to data science, then defense contracting, then — today — principal engineer on an Army personnel system used by 50,000+ people every week, after standing up the Office of Naval Research's first data and analytics division.

Front Lines of Code is what I build on the side: courses for the same fronts I live on daily — developers boxed in by restrictive networks, engineers learning to work with AI as a teammate rather than autocomplete, and parents (mine included) figuring out what AI actually means for their kids. No hype, no magic. Just what's worked, what hasn't, and what I'd tell a colleague over coffee.
